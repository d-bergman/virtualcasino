Stock Market Field GuideLCN / BAWSAQ

How the market breathes

Every pulse updates all listed companies. One company becomes the headline event, then its sector and network ripple outward. The featured company moves the hardest, similar-sector companies tend to follow, and the opposite side of the market may barely twitch.

Business hours run from 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM Central. During those hours, movement is sharper and news has more bite. After hours still moves, but it is more like thin trading: smaller background drift with occasional ugly surprises.

Market phases

The market has weather now. Bull Market favors risk, Bear Market punishes weak hands, Casino Boom boosts casino names, Health Panic makes health stocks violent, Luxury Bubble runs luxury hot, Recession favors defensive names, and Speculative Mania can send tiny companies flying or crashing.

The Casino Financial Times shows the current phase, phase timer, rumors, movers, dividend notices, and executed limit orders.

Market capitalization

  • Nano stocks move hardest and can moon or crash violently.
  • Small stocks react strongly to news and phase pressure.
  • Mid stocks are the balanced baseline.
  • Large and Mega stocks are steadier, but still move on major headlines.

LCN vs BAWSAQ

LCN is the local offline-style exchange. It behaves like the casino world's internal economy: resorts, banks, travel, logistics, and local industry react to in-game news and sector pressure.

BAWSAQ is the hotter online-style exchange. It is more momentum-driven and reacts hard when player-facing companies catch a bid or panic selloff.

Reading your portfolio

  • Portfolio is the current resale value of every share you own.
  • Unrealized P/L is profit or loss before selling. It can vanish if the next pulse turns against you.
  • Trade Ticket previews cost before you buy, and your holdings panel shows gain or loss before you sell.
  • Dividends periodically pay bankroll for income-style companies you hold.

Automation tools

  • Watchlist lets you favorite companies and filter the market down to only the names you care about.
  • Limit Orders auto-buy when a stock drops to your target or auto-sell when it rises to your target.
  • Orders execute on stock pulses, check bankroll/shares first, and write normal stock history entries.

Sector funds

Funds are bundle investments. Instead of betting on one company, you can buy a unit of a basket like Casino Fund, Health Fund, Tech Growth Fund, Luxury & Travel Fund, or Defensive Fund.

Fund values update from their underlying companies and count toward your portfolio value.

Rumors and headlines

Insider rumors are hints, not promises. Some are confident, some are shaky, and some will be wrong. Use them as a signal, not a guarantee.

Market news has weighted severity and explicit movement ranges, so small headlines should feel different from critical squeezes, approvals, crashes, and sector shocks.

Strategy without getting cooked

  • Buy dips when the company is still healthy, not just because the number is red.
  • Diversify across sectors so one bad casino-tech headline does not wreck the whole wallet.
  • Take profit when a stock jumps hard. Greed is the market's favorite snack.
  • Keep some bankroll liquid for games, assets, and surprise dips.

Craps Quick Guide
Pass Line

Come-out 7 or 11 wins. 2, 3, or 12 loses. Any other number becomes the point, and you win by rolling that point before a 7.

Don't Pass

Almost the opposite: 2 or 3 wins, 7 or 11 loses, 12 pushes. After a point, 7 wins before the point.

Field

One-roll bet. 3, 4, 9, 10, 11 pay even money. 2 pays double. 12 pays triple.

Hardways

Hard 4, 6, 8, or 10 win when rolled as doubles before an easy version or 7 appears.
Blackjack Quick Guide
Goal

Beat the dealer without going over 21. Dealer draws until 17.

Split Pairs

Split only matching ranks. Standard limit is 3 splits, creating up to 4 total hands.

Double Down

Double your hand bet, draw exactly one card, then that hand stands.

XP

Winning hands and special results award Blackjack XP automatically in online play.
