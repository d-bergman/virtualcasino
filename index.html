Stock Market Field GuideLCN / BAWSAQ

How the market breathes

Every pulse updates all listed companies. One company becomes the headline event, then its sector and network ripple outward. The featured company moves the hardest, similar-sector companies tend to follow, and the opposite side of the market may barely twitch.

Business hours run from 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM Central. During those hours, movement is sharper and news has more bite. After hours still moves, but it is more like thin trading: smaller background drift with occasional ugly surprises.

LCN vs BAWSAQ

LCN is the local offline-style exchange. It behaves like the casino world's internal economy: resorts, banks, travel, logistics, and local industry react to in-game news and sector pressure.

BAWSAQ is the hotter online-style exchange. It is more momentum-driven and reacts hard when player-facing companies catch a bid or panic selloff.

Reading your portfolio

  • Portfolio is the current resale value of every share you own.
  • Unrealized P/L is profit or loss before selling. It can vanish if the next pulse turns against you.
  • Trade Ticket previews cost before you buy, and your holdings panel shows gain or loss before you sell.

Strategy without getting cooked

  • Buy dips when the company is still healthy, not just because the number is red.
  • Diversify across sectors so one bad casino-tech headline does not wreck the whole wallet.
  • Take profit when a stock jumps hard. Greed is the market's favorite snack.
  • Keep some bankroll liquid for games, assets, and surprise dips.

Craps Quick Guide
Pass Line

Come-out 7 or 11 wins. 2, 3, or 12 loses. Any other number becomes the point, and you win by rolling that point before a 7.

Don't Pass

Almost the opposite: 2 or 3 wins, 7 or 11 loses, 12 pushes. After a point, 7 wins before the point.

Field

One-roll bet. 3, 4, 9, 10, 11 pay even money. 2 pays double. 12 pays triple.

Hardways

Hard 4, 6, 8, or 10 win when rolled as doubles before an easy version or 7 appears.
Blackjack Quick Guide
Goal

Beat the dealer without going over 21. Dealer draws until 17.

Split Pairs

Split only matching ranks. Standard limit is 3 splits, creating up to 4 total hands.

Double Down

Double your hand bet, draw exactly one card, then that hand stands.

XP

Winning hands and special results award Blackjack XP automatically in online play.
